Americans have a long tradition of supporting an individual’s fundamental right to freedom of expression. Our laws specifically protect individuals from persecution for expressing “unpopular” opinions. While it is understood that the right to free speech will result in some “unsavory” opinions being expressed, a free and open dialogue is seen as the best way of protecting our democratic ideals. For example, the vast majority of Americans strongly disagree with the intolerant attitudes expressed by individuals such as David Duke,[4] yet they support his right to express his opinions – and even to run for elected office.
